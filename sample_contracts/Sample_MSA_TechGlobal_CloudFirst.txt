MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT

This Master Service Agreement ("Agreement") is made effective as of March 1, 2025 ("Effective Date"),

by and between:

TechGlobal Solutions Inc., a corporation organized under the laws of the State of New York, with principal offices at 200 Park Avenue, New York, NY 10166 ("Client")

and

CloudFirst Services LLC, a limited liability company organized under the laws of the State of Delaware, with offices at 1200 Innovation Blvd, Austin, TX 78701 ("Service Provider")

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Client desires to engage Service Provider for cloud migration, managed services, and digital transformation consulting; and

WHEREAS, Service Provider has the capability and expertise to deliver such services;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Parties agree as follows:

1. SERVICES

1.1 The Service Provider shall provide information technology services as described in individual Statements of Work ("SOW") executed under this Agreement.

1.2 Services may include but are not limited to:
(a) Cloud infrastructure migration and management (AWS, Azure, GCP);
(b) Application modernization and containerization;
(c) 24/7 managed services and IT operations support;
(d) Cybersecurity assessment and implementation;
(e) Data analytics and AI/ML platform development.

1.3 Each SOW shall specify the scope, deliverables, timeline, acceptance criteria, and pricing for the particular engagement.

2. TERM

2.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of three (3) years unless earlier terminated in accordance with Section 9.

2.2 This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year terms unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to the expiration of the then-current term.

3. PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 Fees shall be as specified in each SOW.
3.2 Service Provider shall submit invoices monthly in arrears.
3.3 Payment terms: Net 45 from receipt of a proper invoice.
3.4 Late payments shall accrue interest at the rate of 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less.
3.5 Client may dispute any invoice in good faith within fifteen (15) days of receipt. Undisputed amounts must be paid when due.
3.6 All fees are exclusive of applicable taxes. Client shall be responsible for all sales, use, and value-added taxes.

4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

4.1 Pre-Existing IP: All intellectual property owned by either Party prior to this Agreement ("Pre-Existing IP") shall remain the property of such Party.

4.2 Work Product: All deliverables, documentation, and work product created by Service Provider specifically for Client under this Agreement ("Work Product") shall be owned by Client upon full payment of all applicable fees.

4.3 Service Provider Tools: Service Provider retains ownership of all tools, methodologies, frameworks, and pre-existing code ("Service Provider Tools"). Service Provider grants Client a non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free license to use any Service Provider Tools embedded in the Work Product.

4.4 Feedback: Client grants Service Provider a non-exclusive, perpetual license to use any feedback, suggestions, or improvements provided by Client regarding Service Provider's tools and methodologies.

5. CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 Each Party agrees to maintain the confidentiality of the other Party's proprietary and confidential information.
5.2 Confidential Information shall not be disclosed to any third party without prior written consent.
5.3 The obligations of confidentiality shall survive for three (3) years after termination of this Agreement.

6. WARRANTIES

6.1 Service Provider warrants that:
(a) Services will be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards;
(b) All personnel assigned to perform services shall have the requisite skills and qualifications;
(c) Services will comply with all applicable laws and regulations;
(d) Work Product will not infringe any third-party intellectual property rights.

6.2 EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH HEREIN, NEITHER PARTY MAKES ANY WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

7. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

7.1 NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOST PROFITS, LOST DATA, OR BUSINESS INTERRUPTION.

7.2 THE TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY OF EITHER PARTY SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE UNDER THE APPLICABLE SOW IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM.

7.3 The limitations in this Section 7 shall not apply to: (a) breaches of confidentiality obligations; (b) infringement of intellectual property rights; (c) indemnification obligations; or (d) gross negligence or willful misconduct.

8. INDEMNIFICATION

8.1 Service Provider shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Client from any third-party claims arising from:
(a) Service Provider's breach of this Agreement;
(b) Service Provider's negligence or willful misconduct;
(c) Infringement of third-party intellectual property rights by the Work Product;
(d) Violation of applicable laws by Service Provider.

8.2 Client shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Service Provider from any third-party claims arising from:
(a) Client's breach of this Agreement;
(b) Client's use of the Work Product in violation of applicable laws;
(c) Client-provided materials that infringe third-party rights.

9. TERMINATION

9.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement with ninety (90) days written notice.
9.2 Either Party may terminate immediately upon written notice if the other Party:
(a) Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within thirty (30) days of written notice;
(b) Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors.

9.3 Upon termination:
(a) Service Provider shall complete all work in progress under active SOWs or transition as directed;
(b) Client shall pay for all services rendered through the date of termination;
(c) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party's Confidential Information.

10. DATA PROTECTION

10.1 Service Provider shall comply with all applicable data protection laws, including GDPR and CCPA.
10.2 Service Provider shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data.
10.3 Service Provider shall notify Client within 48 hours of discovering any data breach involving Client data.
10.4 A separate Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") shall be executed as an addendum to this Agreement.

11. FORCE MAJEURE

Neither Party shall be liable for delays or failure to perform due to events beyond its reasonable control, including natural disasters, war, terrorism, pandemic, government actions, or failure of telecommunications infrastructure, provided that the affected Party gives prompt notice and uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact.

12. INSURANCE

Service Provider shall maintain:
(a) Commercial General Liability insurance: $2,000,000 per occurrence;
(b) Professional Liability (E&O) insurance: $5,000,000 per claim;
(c) Cyber Liability insurance: $3,000,000 per claim;
(d) Workers' Compensation insurance as required by law.

13. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

13.1 This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of New York.
13.2 Any dispute shall first be submitted to good-faith mediation.
13.3 If mediation fails, disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration under the rules of the American Arbitration Association in New York, NY.

14. ENTIRE AGREEMENT

This Agreement, together with all SOWs and addenda, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding the subject matter herein. No modification shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by both Parties.

Total Estimated Contract Value: $4,500,000 over the initial 3-year term.
